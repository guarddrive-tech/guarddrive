# On-Chain Rules Engine (GuardDrive™ Sovereign Escrow)

This document acts as the **ledger of immutable laws** governing the GuardDrive™ ecosystem on the blockchain. No graphical interface (UI) or backend script can bypass these rules.

## 1. Protocol Identity
- **Framework:** EVM (Solidity) / Anchor (Rust compatible)
- **Legal Typology:** Trustless Escrow for Telemetry SLAs

## 2. State Machine
An on-chain Telemetry Agreement can only exist in one of four mutually exclusive states (`AgreementStatus`):
1. **Formation:** Agreement created, telemetry rules (SLA) locked in, awaiting participants (e.g., driver or logistics operator) to deposit their guarantee.
2. **Active:** Vehicle in transit, contract running. The capital in the Escrow (Performance Deposit) is frozen.
3. **Settled:** SLA compliance verified by GuardTag™ and Symbeon consensus. Capital released back to the driver.
4. **Breached:** Infraction verified by the Symbeon core. Part or all of the funds are transferred to the fleet treasury, and the remainder is refunded.

## 3. Initialization Rules (`init_telemetry_agreement`)
- The Creator must pay the network rent/gas fee to open the escrow vault.
- A secure on-chain vault address is generated. No centralized administrator owns the private key to this vault.
- The SLA rules agreed upon are transformed into a **32-byte Cryptographic Hash (`rule_hash`)**. This hash is stored in the contract, making it impossible to change rules after signing.

## 4. Custody Rules (`secure_deposit`)
- **Financial Immutability:** The participant must deposit exactly the `deposit_amount` defined at creation. The blockchain rejects partial or divergent deposits.
- Custody moves completely from the user's wallet into the Escrow Vault.

## 5. Performance Settlement Rules (`settle_telemetry_agreement`)
The most critical and shielded instruction in the project.

- **State Filter:** The contract will fail (panic) if an attempt is made to settle an already `Settled` or `Breached` agreement. There is no double-spend or double-penalty.
- **The Trinity Consensus Rule (Multi-Sig BFT):**
  For the contract to release or settle the funds, the transaction must be signed by the **Trinity Consensus Core** (representing the physical hardware attestation and the AI audit verification).
- **Proof Hash:** Settlement only occurs upon the attachment of a `proof_hash`, which is the final cryptographic proof generated by the **Symbeon ZK-circuit** proving that real-world telematic limits were respected. If the signatures or proofs do not match, the settlement is rejected.

## 6. Arbitration Precedents
Under no circumstances can Escrow capital be moved without the express permission of the BFT consensus from the Symbeon nodes. This ensures GuardDrive™ does not act as a custodian, but purely as a Code-is-Law infrastructure.
